Toronto Allergists

Dr. Jason K. Lee, Dr. Vy Kim and Dr. Audrey Olivia Segal makeup the Toronto Allergists. The Toronto Allergists are board-certified in Clinical Immunology and Allergy. The allergists are experts in immunotherapy for the treatment of allergic diseases and allergic asthma. In Toronto Allergists' practice, only the latest evidence based guidelines in strict compliance with the AAAAI and CSACI practice parameters and manuals are used for the treatment of patients.

Dr. Jason K. Lee

Clinical Immunology and Allergy and Internal Medicine

Dr. Jason K Lee is a medical doctor who is a Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada Specialist. He holds two Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians of Canada specialties (FRCPC) in Clinical Immunology and Allergy and Internal Medicine. Dr. Jason K Lee is actively involved in clinical research and teaching of medical students, residents, and fellows at the University of Toronto.

Dr. J Lee is a member of the Ontario Medical Association and College of Physicians and Surgeons of Ontario. He maintains an active membership in and regularly attends conferences and educational updates from: The Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology Society of Ontario (AAISO), The Canadian Society of Allergy and Clinical Immunology (CSACI), The American Academy of Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology (AAAAI), The American College of Allergy, Asthma, and Immunology (ACAAI), and The World Allergy Organization (WAO).

Dr. Vy Kim is a Clinical Immunologist and Allergist who is certified by the Royal College of Physicians of Canada in Pediatrics and Clinical Immunology and Allergy (FRCPC). She completed her medical degree at McMaster University. She then completed her postgraduate training in Pediatrics and Clinical Immunology and Allergy at the Hospital for Sick Children at the University of Toronto.

She currently works in the Division of Immunology and Allergy at the Hospital for Sick Children and in her private practice with her practice partner Dr. J Lee. Her particular interests include primary immunodeficiencies, bone marrow transplantation, food allergies, drug allergies, vaccine reactions, anaphylaxis, urticaria, asthma, allergic rhinitis and rhinosinusitis, atopic dermatitis, systemic mastocytosis and medical education.
